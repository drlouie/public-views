            Intel&reg; PRO/Wireless 2011 LAN Access Point </b><b><br>
            </b> <font color="#333366"><br>
            802.11b Wireless access point with 128-bit WEP<br>
            <br>
            Functioning as a wireless hub, the Intel® PRO/Wireless 2011 LAN Access 
            Point serves as a bridge between an existing wired network and wireless 
            devices. In addition, the access point can act as a repeater, passing 
            along transmissions from one access point to another, creating a wireless 
            backbone and connecting hard-to-reach areas. Intel wireless access 
            points are simple to install, since they ship with a site survey tool 
            to assist in proper placement for desired coverage and performance. 
            IT administrators can manage and monitor the network of access points 
            remotely via standard web browser. <br>
            <br>
            Ethernet-speed connectivity <br>
            Up to 11Mbps throughput provides reliable access to email, Internet, 
            file sharing and other network resources. The Intel PRO/Wireless 2011 
            LAN Access Point always seeks to connect at 11Mbps and then switches 
            to 5.5, 2 or 1, as network traffic demands, for increased signal range 
            - up to 500 meters, for broad roaming capabilities. <br>
            <br>
            Seamless roaming <br>
            Dynamic rate scaling, IP mobile functionality and advanced transmit/receive 
            technology allow multiple Intel PRO/Wireless 2011 LAN Access Points 
            to serve users on the go. The Access Points automatically adjust connection 
            speeds according to traffic demands, assuring mobile workers and students 
            optimal performance with reliable network access. Omnidirectional 
            (360 degrees) and directional (20-90 degrees) signal control allows 
            Intel PRO/Wireless 2011 LAN Access Points to be configured for specific 
            power and wiring requirements. <br>
            <br>
            Solves wiring concerns <br>
            In some locations - conference rooms, temporary classrooms, or information 
            kiosks, for example - network access may be desirable, but network 
            wiring is impractical. Intel PRO/Wireless Access Points can be strategically 
            located, out of the way, to provide LAN access while avoiding the 
            time and expense of wiring. <br>
            <br>
            IEEE 802.11b High Rate compliant <br>
            Intel PRO/Wireless Access Points are designed to interoperate with 
            standards-based 11Mbps Wi-Fi* approved products, and are backward 
            compatible with 802.11 direct sequence products at 1 and 2 Mbps, enabling 
            IT administrators to choose best-in-class solutions and protect their 
            investment in existing infrastructure. And, standards-based architecture 
            future-proofs your system by ensuring continued industry support. 
            <br>
            <br>
            Ease of manageability <br>

            Support for SNMP and an embedded HTTP server enable IT administrators 
            to configure, monitor, and upgrade Access Points from anywhere in 
            the world using a standard web browser. A Site Survey utility assists 
            in finding the most effective placement for Access Points to deliver 
            maximum coverage. <br>
