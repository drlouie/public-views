Catch The New Wave Of Light.

You'll be doing the environment a favor when you switch to compact fluorescents. Each incandescent replaced with a compact fluorescent saves the energy equivalent of about 46 gallons of oil or a ton of carbon dioxide emissions over the life of the bulb.

And consider this: The lighting output of each compact fluorescent equals nine incandescents. That means at the end of a compact fluorescent's life, there will have been eight less bulbs to throw out and eight less bulbs to make their way to our overfilled landfills.

Switching an incandescent for a fluorescent can save you an easy $32.50 over the course of 10,000 hours of use. Here's how:

  • To begin with, you'll have to replace the incandescent nine times to match the life expectancy of a single compact fluorescent. At $.75 a piece x 9 times, you'll save $6,75 by using a compact.
  • Energy costs at ten cents per kWh amount to $15 for the compact fluorescent (15 watts x $.10 x 10,000 hours), $60 for the incandescents (60 watts x $.10 x 10,000 hours)
  • Add 'em up. Even with the higher acquisition price, the compact fluorescent comes out the winner: $35 compared to $67.50. That's a savings of over $32.50 over the life of each bulb.

Did you know?

Light output is actually measured in lumens and not in watts. To save energy, find the bulb with the light output you need (lumens), then choose the one with the lowest watts.
