Background

How Well Do You Really Know Your Halogen Torchiere?

For many of us, the halogen torchiere is like an old friend. You may have met in college, and traveled together to your first apartment. Now, the two of you may share an office-either at home or at work. Across the country, an amazing 50 million torchiere-style halogen floor lamps are in use one for

every two households! Many people have two or three. These graceful lamps are versatile enough to bring the brilliance of the midday sun to the darkest room, or provide just the right touch of ambiance with a soft, amber glow...all for a mere $20, $15, or even $10 apiece.

Unfortunately, there is a catch. That "warm glow" is a lot warmer than you'd probably think! As this infrared photograph illustrates, the halogen torchiere on the right gets far hotter than the fluorescent model next to it, yet produces a similar amount of useful light.

Fire, Safety and Energy Issues

The Heat Is On.

The bulb in your halogen torchiere can get as hot as 700 to 1,100° F. That's hot enough to burn paper, cloth, wood and even plastic. In fact, a halogen bulb is so hot that it can cook an egg in three to five minutes. (Don't try this at home!) This extreme heat isn't simply surprising--it's dangerous.

Halogen torchieres already have caused 350 fires, 32 deaths, and 114 injuries in the U.S. alone. Why do these bulbs get so hot? Because they guzzle electricity. Every ten watts of power consumed by a halogen torchiere bulb yield nine watts of heat and only one watt of visible light. No wonder these lamps require 300 or even 500 watts to light a room.

New halogen torchieres are equipped with an ugly metal cage intended to keep the hazardous bulb away from flammable items. Although it improves the safety of the old products, it misses the real point: Why cover up a dangerous light source when you can eliminate the problem entirely--and save money, too?

As Temperatures Rise, So Does Your Energy Bill.

When you waste that much energy, you're also wasting a lot of money--more each year than you paid for the fixture to begin with. A typical halogen torchiere can cost as much as $25 to $100 per year to operate. So...that lamp of yours isn't such a great bargain, after all!

Our Involvement

The LightSite Quest: Finding A Better Alternative to Halogen Torchieres

December, 1994 - Building The Prototype
Chris Calwell, a founder of Ecos Consulting and member of the LightSite team began his study of the halogen torchiere while working at the Natural Resources Defense Council in 1994. He was helping the California utilities design and coordinate their rebate programs for energy efficient compact fluorescent bulbs. He soon discovered that high wattage halogen torchieres were selling in rapidly increasing numbers in home centers and discount stores. Recognizing that their 300 to 600 watt lamps were rapidly eroding all of the energy savings that had been achieved with compact fluorescent bulbs, he resolved to find a solution. In December 1994, he and Jon Koomey, a Lawrence Berkeley Laboratory (LBNL) scientist, built the first three way compact fluorescent prototype torchiere in Jon's workshop. The prototype was a jumble of wires, electrical tape, and Velcro, but it worked, proving the viability of the concept.

1995 - Spreading The Word
Chris demonstrated the fixture at a national lighting meeting in early 1995, and then embarked on a systematic effort to convince utilities, government agencies, and manufacturers of the need for more efficient, safer alternatives to halogen torchieres. Michael Siminovitch and Erik Page of LBNL took up the challenge as well, conducting vital original research to design and test attractive, efficient, compact fluorescent torchieres.

1995-1997 - Doing The Legwork
From 1995 to 1997, Chris conducted the first research into the nationwide sales, energy consumption, and safety record of halogen torchieres. Then he published his findings in two ground-breaking reports for E SOURCE, a Home Energy cover story, and an IAEEL newsletter cover story. His original Home Energy magazine cover photo of an egg frying on a halogen torchiere has been widely reprinted and duplicated for other media events, and he has worked with numerous North American TV stations and the Wall Street Journal to get out the word about torchieres.

1996-1997 - Achieving ENERGY STAR Compliance
In 1996 and 1997, Chris worked with his Ecos colleagues to help EPA develop an ENERGY STAR label for high quality, energy efficient light fixtures, so it would be easier for consumers to identify and purchase the products. While teaching at Colby College in early 1997, Chris developed an energy curriculum in which his students measured torchiere usage in the residence halls and successfully petitioned campus administrators to ban the products. For the last three years, Chris has traveled throughout the U.S. and Europe, giving lectures and multimedia presentations about torchieres.

1998 - Turning The Public On
In 1998, Chris and the Ecos team conducted the first nationwide survey of halogen torchiere use on college campuses, and built the first comprehensive national database of torchiere fires, deaths, injuries, and property damage. Chris recently helped conduct a highly successful torchiere turn in event in Milwaukee, during which 690 ENERGY STAR torchieres were purchased by the public in 90 minutes and 813 halogen torchieres were recycled over a period of 7 hours. The event was covered by three local TV stations and subsequently replicated by Home Depot throughout the Milwaukee area.

Today - Building The Ultimate Online Light Source
Ecos Consulting now operates ENERGY STAR torchiere incentive and promotion programs on behalf of utilities in six states. Ecos launched LightSite jointly with Energy Federation Incorporated to complement those programs, making the products available at reasonable prices to anyone in the country. LightSite uses the power of the Internet to bring information and utility incentives to all qualifying customers as well, without the traditional hassles of paper-based coupons and rebate forms.

 

Halogen vs. Energy Star

Make The Brighter Choice.

More than 50 million halogen torchieres have been sold in the U.S. since the late 1980's.

  • Halogen torchieres have caused at least 350 fires, 114 injuries, 30 deaths, and $2,000,000 in property damage since 1991, yet they can still be legally sold in this country.
  • 1 out of 6 universities have experienced fires caused by halogen torchieres. Two-thirds of the colleges in the country have banned their use in residence halls because of safety concerns.
  • Halogen torchieres run 6 to 8 times hotter than ENERGY STAR torchieres -- hot enough to burn paper, fabric, wood, and even plastic.
  • America spends about $1.3 billion a year on the electricity to operate halogen torchieres. This is equivalent to the annual output of four massive coal-fired power plants.
  • Halogen torchiere electricity use has wiped out all of the energy savings nationwide from using compact fluorescent light bulbs.
  • Torchiere electricity use causes about 12 million tons of carbon dioxide emissions each year -- as much as 22 billion miles of driving.
  • ENERGY STAR torchieres save about $30 to $60 apiece each year in energy bills, returning more money per dollar invested in their purchase than savings accounts, mutual funds, or stocks.
